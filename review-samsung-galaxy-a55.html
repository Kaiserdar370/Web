Samsung Galaxy A55 5G Review: Premium Build Without the Premium Price

👩‍🔬
Priya Nair
Budget & Mid-Range Expert
📅 December 28, 2024 ⏱️ 7 min read
★★★★8.2/10
💎

Samsung has a habit of putting premium design and build features into mid-range devices while keeping the specs modest. The Galaxy A55 continues that tradition — and for buyers who prioritize build quality and Samsung's ecosystem, it delivers real value.

Quick Take: The Galaxy A55 5G is the best-built mid-range phone Samsung has ever made. IP67 water resistance, Gorilla Glass Victus+ on both sides, and a near-flagship camera at ₹38,999. The Exynos 1480 chip is adequate rather than impressive, but the overall package is genuinely compelling.

Build Quality: This Is What ₹40K Should Feel Like

The Galaxy A55 5G has an IP67 rating — officially water resistant to 1 metre for 30 minutes. Gorilla Glass Victus+ on the front and back provides serious drop and scratch protection. The aluminum frame completes a trio of features you'd typically expect to pay ₹70,000+ for. Samsung has democratized premium build quality in a meaningful way here. The phone feels expensive. In the Awesome Navy color I tested, it looks striking.

Camera and Software: Samsung Consistency

The 50MP main camera produces Samsung-quality images that are well-processed, slightly saturated (in a pleasing way), and sharp in daylight. The 12MP ultrawide is better than you'd expect at this price — useful for landscapes and architecture. Portrait mode has that Samsung smoothness that users love. Low light is acceptable but not exceptional. One UI 6.1 on Android 14 is Samsung's most refined software yet. The promise of 4 years of OS updates and 5 years of security patches is the best in the mid-range segment.

What We Liked

  • IP67 water resistance is rare at this price
  • Gorilla Glass Victus+ front and back
  • Near-flagship camera quality
  • One UI 6.1 is polished and feature-rich
  • 4 years OS updates + 5 years security patches
  • Premium build that feels expensive

What Could Be Better

  • Exynos 1480 performance is mid-range, not impressive
  • 25W charging is slow in 2024
  • No telephoto camera
  • Some Samsung apps you can't uninstall
  • Slightly expensive vs some Chinese rivals
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The Galaxy A55 5G is Samsung's best mid-range argument in years. It proves you don't need to spend ₹80,000 for IP67, Gorilla Glass Victus+, and a capable camera. If brand reliability, build quality, and software longevity matter more to you than raw specifications, the A55 is a very smart buy.

✅ Buy If: Build quality and durability matter to you ✅ Buy If: Long software support is a priority ❌ Skip If: Raw performance or fast charging matter ❌ Skip If: You want the best camera under ₹40K
