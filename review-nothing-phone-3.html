Nothing Phone (3) Review: Design Disruption or Expensive Gimmick?

👨‍💻
Arjun Mehta
Senior Tech Reviewer
📅 January 7, 2025 ⏱️ 8 min read
★★★★8.1/10
⬜

Nothing is one of the most interesting stories in consumer tech. Carl Pei's company has built a genuine fanbase around transparent design and a philosophy that phones should be more human. The Phone (3) is their most ambitious device yet.

Quick Take: The Nothing Phone (3) is genuinely unique in a market of increasingly similar-looking flagships. The Glyph Interface has matured into something actually useful. The cameras are much improved. But at ₹54,999, you're paying a premium for design philosophy that not everyone will connect with.

Glyph Interface 2.0: More Than a Party Trick Now

The LED lighting system on the back of the Phone (3) has evolved significantly. Beyond the notification patterns and charging indicators of earlier models, you now have Glyph Timer (set countdown timers visible on the back), Glyph Progress (visualize app progress like food deliveries), and an improved Essential Notifications system that lets you assign specific Glyph patterns to important contacts. After two weeks of use, I found myself actually relying on the Glyph for certain notifications — particularly for calls while my phone was face-down on my desk. That's a success.

Cameras: Finally Competitive

Previous Nothing phones had average cameras. The Phone (3) changes that story. The 50MP main sensor produces detailed, well-exposed images in daylight that can stand alongside mid-range competitors. The 50MP ultrawide with autofocus is genuinely versatile — macro shots are impressive. Portrait mode has improved edge detection. Low-light performance is acceptable but not exceptional. This is the first Nothing phone where I'd say the camera is genuinely good rather than merely adequate.

What We Liked

  • Glyph Interface is now functionally useful, not just aesthetic
  • Unique design that stands out from every other phone
  • Cameras are the best Nothing has ever made
  • Clean NothingOS with minimal bloatware
  • Build quality is excellent
  • 65W charging is fast enough

What Could Be Better

  • ₹54,999 is a lot for Snapdragon 8s Gen 3 (not flagship chip)
  • No IP rating
  • Camera still can't match rivals at similar price points
  • Glyph novelty fades for some users
  • Limited software update track record so far
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The Nothing Phone (3) is the right phone for a specific kind of buyer: someone who cares deeply about design differentiation, wants a clean software experience, and values standing out from the crowd. For pure spec-per-rupee, other phones at ₹55K offer more. But no other phone at any price looks and feels like this.

✅ Buy If: Design uniqueness matters to you ✅ Buy If: You value clean, bloatware-free software ❌ Skip If: You want flagship chip performance ❌ Skip If: Camera quality is your top priority
