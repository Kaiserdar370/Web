Realme GT 6 Review: 120Hz AMOLED Gaming Beast Under ₹35,000

👨‍🎮
Rahul Singh
Gaming & Performance
📅 January 9, 2025 ⏱️ 7 min read
★★★★8.3/10
🎮

I'm a competitive mobile gamer. I test phones with real game sessions — not synthetic benchmarks — because that's what actually matters. The Realme GT 6 surprised me. At ₹34,999, it offers gaming performance I'd expect to pay ₹60,000 for.

Quick Take: If mobile gaming is your primary use case and you have a ₹35,000 budget, the Realme GT 6 is a no-brainer. The Snapdragon 8s Gen 3 handles every title I threw at it. The 120W charging keeps you in the game. The camera is the expected compromise at this price.

Gaming Performance: Snapdragon 8s Gen 3 Delivers

I ran the GT 6 through BGMI, Call of Duty: Mobile, Genshin Impact, Asphalt 9, and Real Racing 3 — all at maximum settings over multiple sessions. BGMI hit consistent 90fps with no thermal throttling in the first 30 minutes. Genshin Impact at highest settings held 58-60fps for the first 25 minutes before a slight dip to 52fps — still very playable. The phone ran warm but not uncomfortably hot. The dedicated gaming mode (GT Mode) showed a genuine 8-10% performance improvement in sustained workloads. For a sub-₹35K phone, this is extraordinary gaming capability.

Display and 120W Charging

The 6.78-inch AMOLED panel with 120Hz refresh and up to 2160Hz touch sampling rate (in gaming mode) makes inputs feel immediate and responsive. Touch latency is genuinely low — in FPS games, this matters. The 120W SuperVOOC charging fills the 5500mAh battery in 42 minutes. Combined with the large battery capacity, you rarely need to think about charging during a gaming session. Battery life on moderate days extends to a comfortable day and a half.

What We Liked

  • Snapdragon 8s Gen 3 delivers near-flagship gaming
  • 120W charging on a 5500mAh battery is excellent
  • 120Hz AMOLED with ultra-low touch latency
  • Gaming Mode with real performance uplift
  • Competitive price for performance level
  • 4 years security updates promised

What Could Be Better

  • Camera system is below average — clear compromise area
  • Build feels slightly plasticky compared to premium rivals
  • OEM UI (Realme UI) has noticeable bloatware
  • Speakers are single-channel, not stereo
  • No IP rating
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The Realme GT 6 is the best gaming phone under ₹35,000 in India right now. It's not a balanced all-rounder — the camera system shows where corners were cut — but if gaming performance is your priority, no phone at this price competes. The 120W charging is a bonus that flagship users would appreciate.

✅ Buy If: Mobile gaming is your primary use case ✅ Buy If: You want flagship gaming under ₹35K ❌ Skip If: Camera quality matters to you ❌ Skip If: You want a balanced all-rounder
