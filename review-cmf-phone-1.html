CMF Phone 1 Review: Nothing's ₹15,999 Modular Surprise

👩‍🔬
Priya Nair
Budget & Mid-Range Expert
📅 December 12, 2024 ⏱️ 6 min read
★★★★8.1/10
🔧

CMF is Nothing's sub-brand, targeted at younger buyers with tighter budgets. The Phone 1 is their debut device, and it arrives with a genuinely clever design hook: modular, swappable back covers secured with a visible screw system.

Quick Take: At ₹15,999, the CMF Phone 1 offers Nothing's DNA — clean software, distinctive design, genuine quality — at half the price. It's not perfect, but for first-time smartphone buyers or anyone wanting a solid budget phone with personality, it's remarkable value.

The Modular Design: Gimmick or Genuinely Useful?

The Phone 1's back cover can be removed with a standard screwdriver and swapped for accessory backs including a card holder, a stand, and decorative color options. In practice, I found the card holder genuinely useful — I attached my travel card to the back and stopped carrying a wallet for short trips. The stand attachment is convenient for video calls. Whether you use the modularity beyond the default back cover depends on your habits, but unlike some gimmicks, this one has tangible daily utility. The design itself carries Nothing DNA — clean, minimal, with the distinctive Nothing typeface on hardware elements.

Performance and Software at ₹15,999

The Dimensity 7300 chip runs Nothing OS 2.6 (based on Android 14) with impressive smoothness. App opens are fast, multitasking handles daily tasks without drama, and BGMI runs on medium settings at a playable framerate. This is not a gaming phone, but it handles the basics well. Nothing OS is the best part of the package — minimal bloatware, clean notifications, and a design language that makes the phone feel premium despite its price. Two years of Android OS updates and three years of security patches is adequate for a budget device.

What We Liked

  • Modular design is genuinely useful, not just aesthetic
  • Nothing OS is clean, bloatware-free, and polished
  • 50MP camera is good for daylight at this price
  • 120Hz AMOLED display is premium for the segment
  • Excellent value for ₹15,999
  • Nothing's design DNA at an accessible price

What Could Be Better

  • Camera falls apart in low light
  • 33W charging is slow
  • No wireless charging
  • 2MP depth sensor is essentially useless
  • Only 2 years OS updates
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The CMF Phone 1 punches above its weight in design and software, and that combination makes it the most interesting budget phone in India. If you're buying your first good smartphone or want to upgrade from an older sub-₹10K device, this delivers quality that will genuinely surprise you.

✅ Buy If: You want quality design under ₹16,000 ✅ Buy If: Modular accessories appeal to you ❌ Skip If: Photography is important to you ❌ Skip If: Gaming performance matters
