iPhone 15 Pro Max Review: Apple's Best Smartphone Yet — But Should You Upgrade?

👨‍💻
Arjun Mehta
Senior Tech Reviewer
📅 January 10, 2025 ⏱️ 9 min read
★★★★★9.5/10
📱

Let me be upfront about something: I've been reviewing phones for eight years, and I've seen Apple iterate cautiously year after year. The iPhone 15 Pro Max is different. After using it as my primary device for a full month — replacing my long-time daily driver — I can say this is the first iPhone in years that actually made me rethink how I use a smartphone.

That doesn't mean it's perfect. Far from it. There are aspects of this phone that genuinely frustrated me, and I'll be completely honest about those. But if you're considering spending ₹1,59,900 on a smartphone, you deserve a thorough, honest evaluation — not a spec-sheet recap dressed up as a review.

Bottom Line Up Front: The iPhone 15 Pro Max is the most complete smartphone Apple has ever made. The new titanium build, the A17 Pro chip, and — most surprisingly — the camera system make it worth considering even if you own an iPhone 14 Pro Max. But the price remains steep, and Android rivals are closing the gap fast.

Design & Build: Titanium Changes Everything

The shift from stainless steel to titanium might sound like a marketing talking point, but you genuinely feel it the moment you pick up the phone. The iPhone 15 Pro Max weighs 221g — noticeably lighter than the 240g of last year's model. After a full day of use, your hand, arm, and shoulder actually notice the difference.

The brushed titanium finish looks incredible in Natural Titanium (the color I reviewed). It's more subdued than previous iPhones — almost tool-like in its restraint, which I personally love. The frosted back glass is unchanged and still collects fingerprints enthusiastically, but the overall package feels more premium than ever.

The Action Button replacing the mute switch is genuinely useful once you customize it. I mapped mine to launch the camera, and I used it constantly. My one complaint: in the dark or without looking, it's sometimes hard to find quickly. A bit more tactile feedback would help.

The USB-C switch is long overdue. The port supports USB 3.0 speeds on the Pro, so transferring 4K ProRes footage to a laptop is actually fast now. Regular users will probably never notice. Video creators will be relieved.

Display: Still One of the Best Screens on Any Phone

The 6.7-inch Super Retina XDR OLED display pushes up to 2000 nits peak brightness, and on a sunny afternoon in Mumbai, I could see everything clearly while others around me were shielding their screens. The 120Hz ProMotion is as smooth as ever — scrolling through articles, swiping through photos, using the Dynamic Island all feel luxurious.

I'll be honest: I can no longer tell the difference between Apple's display and Samsung's Galaxy S24 Ultra's display in day-to-day use. Both are exceptional. Where Apple still wins is in color accuracy for professional work — designers and video editors will appreciate the consistency.

Performance: A17 Pro Is Overkill (In the Best Way)

The A17 Pro chip is built on TSMC's 3nm process, and it shows. Everything is instantaneous. Apps open before you've finished tapping. Multitasking between complex apps is seamless. But what really impressed me were the gaming benchmarks — and more importantly, the real-world gaming experience.

I played Genshin Impact for 45 minutes straight at maximum settings. The phone got warm but never hot. The frame rate stayed locked at 60fps throughout. This is what hardware headroom looks like: the chip isn't just fast, it's so far ahead that today's apps barely stress it.

The 8GB of RAM ensures that apps I'd opened hours ago were still in memory when I came back to them. I never experienced an app reload during my testing period. Never.

Camera System: The Real Star of the Show

The 48MP main camera, 12MP ultrawide, and — the headliner — the new 5x tetraprism periscope telephoto lens. That last one deserves real attention. Previous iPhone Pro models had a 3x optical zoom. The jump to 5x changes what you can actually capture with a phone camera.

Portraits at 5x are stunning. The subject isolation, the bokeh rendering, the skin tones — I compared shots side by side with a Canon EOS R6 at equivalent focal lengths, and while the DSLR wins on fine detail, the iPhone holds its ground impressively. For street photography or travel, this phone is genuinely capable.

Low-light photography has improved meaningfully. The Night mode is faster and captures more detail than the 14 Pro Max. I shot a busy street scene at 11pm with almost no artificial lighting — the iPhone produced a usable, natural-looking image while my Pixel 8 Pro (which also produced an excellent shot) leaned more heavily on processing to brighten shadows.

Video is where Apple still leads. ProRes Log capture at 4K/60fps directly to an external SSD via USB-C is a feature filmmakers have been waiting years for. For most users, this is irrelevant. For the creative professionals Apple is targeting with the Pro line, it's genuinely revolutionary.

Battery Life: Decent, Not Great

This is where Apple continues to frustrate me. The 4,422mAh battery gets me through a full day — but only just. Heavy users who travel, use maps, and shoot lots of video will find themselves reaching for a charger by 8pm. By comparison, the Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra and the OnePlus 13 both outlast the iPhone on a standard day of use.

The 27W wired charging is Apple's biggest remaining embarrassment. In 2025, when competitors charge at 80W, 100W, even 125W, Apple's flagship takes over 90 minutes to charge from zero. The MagSafe wireless charging at 15W is convenient but slow. If you're upgrading from an older iPhone, get used to plugging in at night — and don't forget to actually do it.

Software: iOS 17 Is Polished, But Control Center Still Annoys

iOS 17 is the most mature version of iOS yet. Standby mode for charging is genuinely useful. NameDrop for sharing contacts is clever. The interactive widgets work well. The App Library, Spotlight Search, and Focus modes have all been refined over years of iteration.

What still bothers me, and this is personal, is the lack of customization freedom. Android users can do things with their home screen and notification system that iPhone users simply cannot. If that matters to you, no version of iOS will fix it. If you're already in the Apple ecosystem and love it, you'll find iOS 17 a very comfortable home.

What We Liked

  • Titanium build feels premium and significantly lighter than steel
  • 5x periscope telephoto is a genuine upgrade over 3x
  • A17 Pro chip offers years of performance headroom
  • ProRes video at 4K/60fps is a filmmaking tool in your pocket
  • USB-C finally arrives with USB 3 speeds on Pro
  • Action Button is genuinely useful once customized
  • 6+ years of guaranteed software updates

What Could Be Better

  • 27W wired charging is embarrassingly slow at this price
  • Battery life is merely adequate, not impressive
  • Starting at ₹1,59,900, it's a luxury product
  • iOS customization lags far behind Android
  • No major design change from iPhone 14 Pro Max
  • Box no longer includes USB-C cable of sufficient length
🏆 FINAL VERDICT

The iPhone 15 Pro Max earns its 9.5/10 not because it's perfect — it isn't — but because it's the most polished and capable smartphone package Apple has ever assembled. The titanium build, the extraordinary 5x telephoto camera, the A17 Pro chip's jaw-dropping performance, and the promise of years of software updates make it worth the premium for those who live in the Apple ecosystem.

If you're on an iPhone 13 or older, upgrade without hesitation. On an iPhone 14 Pro Max, the telephoto and titanium are real improvements, but you won't feel like you're missing out either. Android users considering a switch will get the best iOS experience ever — but the charging speeds and price will sting.

✅ Buy If: You want Apple's best ✅ Buy If: You shoot lots of video ❌ Skip If: Fast charging matters to you ❌ Skip If: Budget is under ₹1.5L
