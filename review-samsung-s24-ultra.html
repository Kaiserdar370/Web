Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra Review: The S Pen Powerhouse That Got Smarter

👨‍💻
Arjun Mehta
Senior Tech Reviewer
📅 January 8, 2025 ⏱️ 10 min read
★★★★★9.2/10
🖊️

Samsung's S-series has always been the Android flagship benchmark, and the S24 Ultra arrives with two significant additions: a titanium frame that replaces the previous armour aluminum, and Galaxy AI — a suite of on-device and cloud-powered AI features that Samsung is betting their future on. After a month of daily use, I have opinions on both.

Let me start with the bottom line: the S24 Ultra is the best Android phone you can buy right now. It's not perfect, and I'll tell you exactly where it falls short. But for pure capability, nothing on the Android side matches this package.

Quick Take: The Galaxy S24 Ultra is the most complete Android smartphone available. The titanium frame is a meaningful upgrade, Galaxy AI features are genuinely useful (not just gimmicks), and the 200MP camera system continues to impress. Battery life is exceptional. The S Pen remains the most useful stylus on any phone. Price is steep but justified.

Design & Build: Titanium Arrives on Android Too

The switch to a titanium frame (same move Apple made with the iPhone 15 Pro) results in a phone that's marginally lighter than the S23 Ultra while feeling equally robust. The flat display is back after the curved edges of previous generations — and I couldn't be happier about it. Flat screens are more practical for everyday use and S Pen input, and they don't trigger accidental touches near the edges.

The S24 Ultra is a large phone. At 218.1 × 98.4mm, it won't fit comfortably in smaller hands. If you've never owned an Ultra before, hold one in a store before committing — some people love the size, others find it unwieldy. The titanium sides have a matte, almost industrial finish that I find more sophisticated than shiny metal alternatives.

The integrated S Pen silo remains a key differentiator. Samsung is the only major manufacturer putting a pressure-sensitive stylus inside a flagship phone, and that fact continues to matter for a specific, loyal audience.

Display: Still the Best Screen on Any Android

The 6.8-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X display with 120Hz adaptive refresh is Samsung's finest panel. Peak brightness reaches 2600 nits — slightly less than Apple's 2000 nits claim, but the Samsung panel consistently reads as brighter in outdoor testing because of better ambient light management.

Vision Booster technology makes the screen readable in direct sunlight in a way that impresses every time I use it. Color accuracy is excellent in Natural mode; Default mode is more saturated and punchy, which many users prefer. Anti-reflective coating on the Titanium Black model I reviewed noticeably reduces glare compared to older S-series phones.

Performance: Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 Is a Monster

In India, the S24 Ultra ships with Qualcomm's Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 (exclusive to Samsung for the first few months of 2024). This chip is exceptional. Benchmarks are impressive on paper; real-world performance is what matters, and it delivers. Genshin Impact at maximum settings for an hour generated warmth but no throttling. Call of Duty Mobile at max settings and 120fps ran flawlessly.

The 12GB of RAM ensures aggressive multitasking stays fluid. With 30 tabs open in Chrome, four social apps running, and Spotify in the background, the phone didn't stutter. This is the kind of headroom you pay for in a flagship.

Galaxy AI: Genuinely Useful, Not Just Marketing

Samsung's Galaxy AI features were my biggest skepticism going in and my biggest surprise coming out. Several features are legitimately excellent:

Circle to Search: Press and hold the home button and circle anything on screen to search it with Google. Sounds simple; is actually transformative for quick lookups while watching videos, browsing social media, or reading articles. I use this feature multiple times daily.

Live Translate: Real-time translation during phone calls, displayed on screen for both parties. Tested this on a video call with a colleague speaking German. It worked impressively well with minimal lag.

Note Assist: Automatically summarizes, formats, and translates Samsung Notes. Extremely useful if you use the S Pen for note-taking.

Some features feel more like demos (Generative Edit for photos is novel but I rarely needed it in practice). But the core AI features have meaningfully changed how I use a smartphone, which is something I don't say often.

Camera System: Four Lenses, All Capable

The quad-camera system on the S24 Ultra is remarkable in its versatility. The 200MP main sensor (with pixel-binning to 12.5MP for most shots) produces images with excellent dynamic range and detail. Colors are Samsung-punchy but more restrained than previous generations — a welcome maturation.

The 5x periscope telephoto is excellent — comparable to the iPhone 15 Pro Max's equivalent. The 10x optical zoom works better than I expected for a phone camera, producing sharp images of distant subjects that would have been impossible two years ago. Combined with the 0.6x ultrawide, you have an extraordinarily versatile imaging system.

Video is excellent for 4K content. Log mode for professional video work is available but requires Snapchat-grade processing knowledge to use well. For most users, the auto modes produce impressive, broadcast-quality results.

Battery & Charging

The 5000mAh battery is a genuine all-day performer. On moderate use days (streaming, emails, calls, some photography), I consistently ended the day above 30% — impressive for a power-hungry 6.8-inch display. Heavy gaming dropped that to around 20% by end of day, which is still acceptable.

The 45W charging is faster than Apple's 27W, but still lags behind OnePlus, Xiaomi, and others offering 65W–100W at lower price points. For a ₹1.30 lakh phone, the charging speed is a missed opportunity. The 15W wireless charging is convenient but slow.

Software: One UI 6.1 Is Peak Android

One UI 6.1 on Android 14 is the most refined version of Samsung's software ever. Customization is deep, the DeX desktop mode (when connected to a monitor) is genuinely productive, and the integration between Samsung's ecosystem — Galaxy Watch, Galaxy Buds, Galaxy Tab — is excellent. The 7 years of OS and security update promise is the best in the Android world.

The S Pen software has been refined over a decade. Handwriting recognition in Samsung Notes is excellent. Air Actions for controlling the camera remotely are useful. The pressure sensitivity enables genuine sketching and drawing for creative users.

What We Liked

  • Best Android display available — period
  • Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 is a class-leading performer
  • Galaxy AI features that actually change daily usage
  • 5x periscope telephoto with excellent 10x optical zoom
  • S Pen remains uniquely useful
  • IP68 water resistance
  • 7 years of OS and security updates
  • Exceptional battery life

What Could Be Better

  • 45W wired charging is slow at this price
  • Very large — not suitable for smaller hands
  • Some Galaxy AI features feel gimmicky
  • One UI still has some unnecessary Samsung apps
  • Price starts at ₹1,29,999 — a significant investment
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra earns its 9.2/10 because it is, simply, the most capable Android smartphone money can buy. Extraordinary display, outstanding camera versatility, useful AI features, the unique S Pen, and seven years of software support form a package that no Android competitor currently matches end-to-end.

The price is high. The charging is slower than it should be. But if you're in the market for an Android flagship and want the absolute best the platform offers, the S24 Ultra is your answer.

✅ Buy If: You want the best Android ✅ Buy If: S Pen productivity appeals to you ❌ Skip If: Charging speed is critical ❌ Skip If: You prefer compact phones
