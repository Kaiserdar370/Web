Redmi Note 14 Pro+ Review: The ₹25,000 King Strikes Again

👩‍🔬
Priya Nair
Budget & Mid-Range Expert
📅 January 12, 2025 ⏱️ 8 min read
★★★★½8.7/10
💰

Every year, Xiaomi drops a new Note Pro+ that makes every other phone in the ₹20,000–₹30,000 bracket look inadequate. The Redmi Note 14 Pro+ continues that tradition — but this time, the jump in capability is genuinely impressive, not just incremental.

I've been using the Note 14 Pro+ as my secondary device for three weeks, putting it through the kind of daily abuse that actually matters: dusty commutes, street photography sessions, late-night video calls, and the sort of aggressive multitasking that mid-range processors often choke on. Here's what I found.

Quick Take: The best budget smartphone in India right now, full stop. The camera overachieves, the build quality shocks you at this price, and the 90W charging fills the tank faster than you'll believe. Trade-offs exist — they always do — but none of them are dealbreakers at ₹24,999.

Design: This Doesn't Look Like a Budget Phone

Xiaomi made a bold design decision with the Note 14 Pro+: flat edges, a curved back glass, and a frosted finish that resists fingerprints better than most phones in this price range. Holding it, you wouldn't guess it costs under ₹25,000. The build quality is genuinely solid — not flex-prone, not creaky.

It's available in three colors: Midnight Black, Aurora Purple, and Titan Silver. The Aurora Purple I reviewed has a subtle color-shift effect in the light that genuinely looks premium. The IP64 dust and splash resistance is a real differentiator at this price — you don't usually get that until you spend ₹40,000+.

Display: Bright, Sharp, and Smooth

The 6.67-inch AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate looks excellent in daily use. Peak brightness of 3000 nits means outdoor visibility is a genuine strength — I used this phone on a sunny day and had zero complaints. The curved edges look stylish but occasionally trigger accidental touches; this is a recurring complaint about curved-screen mid-rangers that still hasn't been solved.

Performance: Dimensity 7300 Ultra Delivers

The MediaTek Dimensity 7300 Ultra is not a gaming powerhouse, but it handles everyday tasks with complete confidence. Apps open quickly, the camera app launches fast, and video playback is smooth. BGMI on medium graphics ran without noticeable frame drops in my testing. Genshin Impact at low settings was playable but not comfortable for extended sessions — manage your expectations if gaming is your primary use case.

12GB of RAM keeps apps in memory for hours. I never experienced an app reload during normal usage. The 256GB UFS 3.1 storage is fast and generous at this price point.

Camera: The Headline Feature Earns Its Place

Let's address the 200MP camera claim directly: most of the time, the phone shoots at 12.5MP (pixel-binning 16 pixels into one), which produces sharp, detailed images with good dynamic range. The full 200MP mode exists and works, but produces files over 60MB each — best reserved for when you need to crop aggressively.

Daylight photography is excellent. Colors are vibrant without looking oversaturated, and detail resolution is among the best I've seen in this price bracket. Portrait mode produces pleasing subject separation, though edge detection struggles with complex backgrounds like foliage.

Night mode is where the phone surprises most. In a dimly lit restaurant, it produced clean, natural images that I'd have been proud of on a phone costing twice as much. There's some aggressive noise reduction that softens fine detail, but the exposure handling is excellent.

The ultrawide is good for daylight but noticeably degrades in low light. The 2x optical telephoto (on Pro+ variant) adds useful versatility for portraits and mid-distance subjects. There's no periscope zoom here — that's a flagship feature — but the combination is genuinely capable.

Battery Life: Great Capacity, Blazing Charging

The 5110mAh battery easily lasts a full day with heavy use, and stretches to a day and a half with more moderate use. Streaming, navigation, and camera work are the main drains — if you do all three simultaneously, budget accordingly.

The 90W HyperCharge is genuinely impressive. From empty to 50% in about 15 minutes; full charge in approximately 40 minutes. This is the level of charging speed that changes how you use your phone — you stop worrying about topping up at night and start doing 20-minute charges whenever you have a moment.

Software: HyperOS Has Matured

HyperOS (Xiaomi's replacement for MIUI) running on Android 14 is a significant improvement. The interface is cleaner, animations are smoother, and bloatware has been reduced compared to older MIUI versions. That said, some pre-installed apps still feel unnecessary, and the notification system occasionally shows promotional notifications from Xiaomi apps that you'll want to disable immediately.

Xiaomi promises 2 Android OS updates and 4 years of security patches — adequate, not exceptional. If long software support matters to you, Samsung's A-series and Google Pixels do better.

What We Liked

  • 200MP camera overachieves for the price, especially in daylight
  • IP64 rating is a rare, valuable inclusion under ₹25,000
  • 90W charging fills the 5110mAh battery in ~40 minutes
  • 3000 nit display is excellent outdoors
  • Build quality and design feel premium
  • NFC support for payments
  • 256GB base storage is generous

What Could Be Better

  • Ultrawide camera is merely average
  • No wireless charging at any price tier
  • HyperOS still has some bloatware and promotional notifications
  • Curved edges cause occasional accidental touches
  • Only 2 major Android OS updates promised
  • Gaming performance limited to medium settings for demanding titles
🏆FINAL VERDICT

The Redmi Note 14 Pro+ is the kind of phone that makes you question whether flagship pricing is justified. At ₹24,999, it offers a 200MP camera that produces excellent results, blindingly fast 90W charging, a gorgeous 3000-nit AMOLED display, IP64 water resistance, and a build quality that feels genuinely premium. These aren't marketing claims — they're things you actually experience every day you use this phone.

The trade-offs — limited software support, mediocre ultrawide camera, no wireless charging — are real but feel minor relative to what you're getting for the price. This is our top budget recommendation for India in early 2025.
